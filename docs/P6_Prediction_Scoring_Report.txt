  Every committee member who addressed the direction of H3 predicted A ≥ B.
  Actual: Var(B) > Var(A) on query_rate cross-seed variance (Var ratio = 0.66).

  than local perception. This was outside the model of all five committee members.

  I predicted A < C (emergent beats fixed tax) and A ≈ D (D finds silence
  strategy, producing low ELR without constraint overhead). The actual result
  is the opposite: A < D is robust and A vs C is marginal. D agents learn
  silence is optimal (query_rate=0.305) and achieve high reward (38.93).
  The silence strategy is not exploitation in the gaming sense — it is
  rational cost minimization. I failed to predict this would produce
  significantly higher reward than either constrained condition.
