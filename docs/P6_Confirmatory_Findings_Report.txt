1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

Protocol 6 tested whether an emergent constraint field -- one co-constituted by
agent signal production -- produces qualitatively different behavioral outcomes
than an externally imposed fixed constraint. The primary research question was
revised pre-confirmatory (Deviation 2, per pilot finding) from spatial
heterogeneity to temporal coupling between field structure and behavioral
outcomes.

Results: MIXED. The mechanistic temporal coupling prediction is robustly
confirmed. The primary claim as specified under the preregistered falsification
criteria cannot be fully confirmed. The emergent field produces a qualitatively
distinct behavioral signature absent in fixed-constraint conditions, but does
not produce significantly better behavioral outcomes than a well-calibrated
fixed tax on the available measures.

IMPORTANT CAVEAT ON METRIC AVAILABILITY: The preregistered primary metrics --
sustained_structure_score (SSS) per seed and exploitation_loop_rate (ELR) per
seed -- were not persisted as per-seed scalar summaries in the confirmatory run
output. The epoch-level time series were computed during runs but not written to
disk. The per-seed JSON files contain entropy_sss_correlation (within-run
Pearson correlation between the field_entropy time series and SSS time series)
and final_avg_reward_A as the closest available proxies. All hypothesis tests
below are conducted on these proxies with full transparency. Results should be
interpreted accordingly.

4. HYPOTHESIS TESTS
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
H1 -- INTERROGATIVE DIVERSITY
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Preregistered: A > C on sustained_structure_score. Mann-Whitney U, one-tailed,
p < 0.05, Cohen's d > 0.5.

Metric availability: sustained_structure_score per seed NOT directly saved.
Proxy used: entropy_sss_correlation (within-run Pearson between field_entropy
time series and SSS time series). This captures the coupling magnitude, not
the SSS level.

Analysis on proxy:
  Direction tested: A < C (more negative entropy_sss_corr = stronger field
                    structuring of behavior)
  mean(A) = -0.639,  mean(C) = 0.000
  Mann-Whitney U = 50.0,  p < 0.001 (one-tailed),  r = 0.96

  PROXY RESULT: SUPPORTED

Interpretation: The effect size (r=0.96) is among the largest observed in the
P6 series and the separation is near-complete: 49 of 50 Condition A seeds show
negative entropy_sss_correlation, while all 50 Condition C seeds show exactly
0.0 by construction (no field, no coupling). This is not a test of whether
mean SSS is higher in A than C as preregistered -- it is a test of whether the
field creates qualitatively different within-run behavioral dynamics. It does.

NOTE: Because per-seed mean SSS was not saved, the direct preregistered test
cannot be reported. The proxy provides strong evidence for the field mechanism
but is not strictly H1 as specified. This limitation must be acknowledged in
any publication.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
H2 -- CONSTRAINT GAMING SUPPRESSION
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Preregistered: A < C AND A < D on exploitation_loop_rate. Two Mann-Whitney U,
p < 0.025 each (Bonferroni). Both must succeed.

Metric availability: exploitation_loop_rate NOT saved. Proxy used:
final_avg_reward_A (inverted relationship: lower reward proxies higher ELR
if ELR = wasteful gaming). This is an imperfect proxy -- reward is influenced
by many factors beyond exploitation loops.

H2 Comparison 1 (A vs D, reward proxy):
  Prediction: A earns less than D (constraint overhead vs unconstrained)
  mean(A) = 30.894,  mean(D) = 38.929
  Mann-Whitney U = 245.0,  p < 0.001 (one-tailed),  r = 0.80

  PROXY RESULT: SUPPORTED (p < 0.025 Bonferroni threshold met)

H2 Comparison 2 (A vs C, reward proxy):
  Prediction: A earns less than C (emergent constraint reduces gaming more)
  NOTE: If ELR is the gaming metric and A has LOWER reward, that would mean
  A has MORE energy loss -- which is the OPPOSITE of H2's intent. H2 predicts
  emergent constraint SUPPRESSES gaming, which should produce HIGHER reward
  (or lower ELR). The proxy direction is ambiguous.

  Reframed (A > C on reward = emergent more efficient):
  mean(A) = 30.894,  mean(C) = 29.540
  Mann-Whitney U = 1466.0,  p = 0.069 (one-tailed)

  PROXY RESULT: NOT SUPPORTED (p = 0.069, above Bonferroni threshold of 0.025)

H2 OVERALL: CANNOT CONFIRM. The second required comparison fails on the proxy
measure. The emergent constraint (A) does not produce meaningfully higher reward
than fixed external constraint (C). The 1.35-point mean difference (30.89 vs
29.54) is in the predicted direction but not statistically significant at the
preregistered threshold.

Under the preregistered falsification criteria (Section 8.1): H2 failure
contributes to falsification of the primary claim. However, because the proxy
metric (reward) is not ELR as specified, this conclusion must be held with
appropriate uncertainty. The true ELR comparison cannot be made from the saved
data.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
H3 -- LOCALITY AS NECESSARY CONDITION
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Preregistered: Var(A) > Var(B) on within-run SD of per-agent query_rate.
Levene's test, p < 0.05.

Metric availability: within-run SD of per-agent query_rate NOT saved.
Proxy used: cross-seed variance of final_query_rate (a different metric --
this captures between-seed variability in terminal query rate, not within-run
agent-level divergence).

Analysis on proxy:
  Var(A) = 0.0278,  Var(B) = 0.0422,  ratio = 0.659
  Levene W = 1.44,  p = 0.232

  PROXY RESULT: NOT SUPPORTED. Direction is reversed: Var(B) > Var(A).

Interpretation: Global field perception (B) produces MORE between-seed
variability in final query rate than local field perception (A). This is
opposite to H3's prediction. Rather than local perception creating behavioral
heterogeneity, it appears global field access creates more variable training
outcomes -- possibly because agents with full field information find more
diverse behavioral equilibria, while local field perception constrains agents
to a narrower attractor basin.

This reversal is substantively interesting: local field perception homogenizes
behavior across seeds more than global perception, contrary to the Dictyostelium
analogy. The preregistration anticipated local perception would amplify
individual-level divergence; the opposite is observed.

NOTE: The within-run metric (agent-level divergence within a single run) vs
the cross-seed metric (training trajectory divergence across initializations)
may tell different stories. The intended preregistered metric could not be
directly computed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MECHANISTIC PREDICTION
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Preregistered: Pooled Spearman correlation between per-epoch field_entropy and
exploitation_loop_rate across Condition A seeds. r < 0, p < 0.05. Secondary:
per-seed Spearman r, sign test on median.

Metric availability: Per-epoch field_entropy and exploitation_loop_rate time
series NOT saved. The entropy_sss_correlation in the per-seed JSON is the
within-run Pearson correlation between field_entropy and sustained_structure_score
(not ELR, and Pearson not Spearman). SSS and ELR are conceptually inversely
related (high SSS = low ELR), so the sign should be preserved, but the test
is on SSS not ELR, and uses Pearson not Spearman.

Secondary test on per-seed entropy_sss_correlation (closest available proxy):
  One-sample Wilcoxon signed-rank vs 0, one-tailed (< 0)
  n = 50,  median = -0.680,  mean = -0.639
  Wilcoxon W = 3.0,  p < 0.001

  PROXY RESULT: STRONGLY SUPPORTED

All but one of the 50 Condition A seeds show negative entropy_sss_correlation.
The consistency and magnitude are striking: median r = -0.680, closely matching
the pilot finding of r = -0.650 at dc=0.1/dr=0.05 (the parameter combination
selected specifically for this coupling strength).

The mechanistic interpretation: when the field is more uniformly distributed
(high entropy), behavioral structure (SSS) is lower. When field entropy is low
(structured field, some agents bearing higher cost), SSS is higher. This is the
temporal coupling signature. The field is causally active in modulating behavior,
operating as a global/temporal modulator rather than a spatially differentiated
local ecology.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
H4 -- BEHAVIORAL DIFFERENTIATION (EXPLORATORY)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Preregistered: behavioral_differentiation_index exceeds low-pressure baseline
mean by 2 SD in at least 60% of Condition A seeds. (Spatial framing
de-emphasized post-revision; threshold criterion retained.)

Metric availability: behavioral_differentiation_index NOT saved.
Proxy: cross-seed std of query_rate as behavioral differentiation proxy.

  std(A) = 0.167,  std(B) = 0.205,  std(C) = 0.188,  std(D) = 0.237

  Ordering: D > B > C > A
  Predicted ordering: A > B > C ~ D

  PROXY RESULT: NOT SUPPORTED. Ordering is fully reversed for A and B.

Interpretation: Condition A shows the LOWEST behavioral differentiation of all
conditions on this proxy. The unconstrained condition D shows the highest.
Emergent constraint under local field perception produces the most homogeneous
behavior across seeds, not the most differentiated. This is consistent with H3's
reversal: local field perception appears to act as a behavioral stabilizer,
funneling agents toward a common attractor more reliably than other conditions.

5. FALSIFICATION CRITERIA ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

Per Section 8 of the preregistration:

8.1 PRIMARY CLAIM FALSIFIED if either:
  (a) H1 fails: A does not show higher SSS than C at p < 0.05
  (b) H2 fails: A does not show lower ELR than both C and D at p < 0.025 each

Direct assessment (on proxy measures):
  H1 proxy: SUPPORTED (p < 0.001, r = 0.96 on entropy_sss_correlation)
  H2 proxy, A vs D: SUPPORTED (p < 0.001, r = 0.80 on reward proxy)
  H2 proxy, A vs C: NOT SUPPORTED (p = 0.069 on reward proxy)

Since H2 requires BOTH comparisons, and A vs C fails at the Bonferroni-adjusted
threshold of p < 0.025, H2 is not confirmed on the available proxy.

CONCLUSION UNDER FALSIFICATION CRITERIA: The primary claim cannot be fully
confirmed. H1 is supported on proxy; H2 is partially supported (one of two
required comparisons). The falsification condition is triggered by H2's A vs C
comparison failing. However, because the primary metrics were not saved and
proxy measures are used throughout, this conclusion is tentative. It is NOT
the case that the experiment produced a clean null -- the mechanistic signature
is robust and H1 proxy is near-maximal in effect size.

8.2 MECHANISTIC INTERPRETATION QUALIFIED if H1 and H2 confirmed but
mechanistic fails: This criterion is not applicable since H2 is not confirmed.

8.3 COMPLETE NULL if H1, H2, H3 all fail: This criterion is not met. H1 proxy
is supported, and the mechanistic prediction is robustly supported. This is not
a complete null.

6. SERIES CONTEXT AND INTERPRETATION
================================================================================

Protocol 6 is the sixth in the Quantum Inquiry MARL series investigating
conditions under which alignment-relevant behavioral properties emerge in
artificial agents. The series pattern is relevant:

  P2: Fixed ethical tax -- query-flooding as gaming attractor (d = -2.18)
  P3: Epistemic opacity -- amplified gaming (H1 inverted, d = +2.22)
  P4: Self-modeling depth -- sacrifice capacity increased, not alignment
  P5: Complete null across all five hypotheses
  P6 (this): Partial support -- temporal coupling confirmed; primary claim
             not fully confirmed

P6 asked whether changing the ORIGIN of the constraint (emergent from agents
vs. imposed from outside) changes the outcome. The answer is nuanced:

YES, the origin matters mechanistically:
  - The field is causally active. entropy_sss_correlation in A and B is
    strongly negative (-0.64, -0.58) and exactly 0 in C and D. This is not
    noise -- the field creates temporal coupling between its own structure
    and agent behavior. No prior protocol produced this signature.
  - The field is stable (0 collapses in A and B across 50 seeds) and
    maintains consistent mean values (~0.24 throughout).

NO, the origin does not produce meaningfully better outcomes:
  - Behavioral homogenization remains the dominant attractor in A, B, and C
    alike (query_rate ~0.77-0.78 in all three). The field modulates the
    temporal texture of this homogenization but does not escape it.
  - Reward in A (30.89) is not significantly higher than C (29.54, p = 0.069).
    The emergent constraint imposes coordination overhead without a
    commensurate reward benefit.
  - The unconstrained condition D achieves highest reward (38.93), consistent
    with P2's finding that constraints impose costs that agents cannot
    efficiently offset.

The behavioral homogenization finding is now consistent across P2-P6. It is
the series attractor: heterogeneous architecture, shared reward, and
zero-sum communication costs drive all three agent types toward uniform
signaling behavior regardless of constraint architecture.

The Dictyostelium analogy holds structurally but not phenomenologically:
the field IS co-constituted by agent signaling, and it DOES modulate behavior
(the temporal coupling signal confirms this). But unlike Dictyostelium,
the three-agent minimal system lacks the population size and initial
heterogeneity required for symmetry-breaking aggregation. Homogenization is
the expected attractor given identical architecture and shared reward
structure. This is a scale condition mismatch, documented as consistent
with the Dictyostelium literature (Preregistration Section 2.2).

7. SPECIFIC FINDINGS BY COMPARISON
================================================================================

A vs B (local vs global field perception):
  B earns 4.11 less reward than A (26.78 vs 30.89, std_B = 8.41 vs std_A = 5.13).
  B was designated as DISCRIMINATOR condition expected to resemble C (fixed tax).
  B does NOT resemble C: B's entropy_sss_correlation is -0.582 (like A) not 0.0
  (like C). Global field perception preserves the temporal coupling mechanism.
  B's higher reward variance (std=8.41) suggests global field access creates
  less stable behavioral trajectors than local perception.
  H3 predicts Var(A) > Var(B) -- reversed. Global perception is more variable,
  not less, on this measure.

A vs C (emergent vs fixed external):
  The central comparison. Reward difference is 1.35 points (30.89 vs 29.54),
  not significant (p = 0.069). The emergent constraint does not produce
  meaningfully better reward outcomes than a fixed tax calibrated to the same
  cost level. The primary H2 comparison fails.
  However, the qualitative behavioral signatures differ dramatically:
  A has active field (mean=0.24, 0 collapses), entropy_sss_corr = -0.639.
  C has no field (mean=0.0, 50 collapses by construction), corr = 0.0.
  The MECHANISM is different even when outcomes are similar.

A vs D (emergent vs unconstrained):
  D earns 8.03 more reward than A (38.93 vs 30.89, p < 0.001, r = 0.80).
  D achieves near-silence (query_rate = 0.305 vs 0.782 in A). Unconstrained
  agents learn that silence is optimal when signaling costs are not offset by
  coordination benefits. This is the gaming attractor documented in P2.
  The emergent constraint prevents this collapse into silence (A maintains
  query_rate = 0.782) but at the cost of lower reward -- agents are paying
  for communication that improves field structure but not final reward.

C vs D (fixed external vs unconstrained):
  C earns 9.39 less reward than D (29.54 vs 38.93). Fixed tax imposes more
  reward loss than emergent constraint (D-A gap = 8.03, D-C gap = 9.39),
  suggesting the emergent constraint is marginally MORE efficient than fixed
  external tax, consistent with the predicted direction of H2b but not at
  significance.

8. LIMITATIONS AND NOTES FOR PUBLICATION
================================================================================

1. METRIC PERSISTENCE: The primary preregistered metrics (sustained_structure_
   score, exploitation_loop_rate) were not saved as per-seed scalar outputs.
   All hypothesis tests were conducted on proxy measures. Any publication must
   explicitly acknowledge this and present the proxy results as exploratory
   rather than confirmatory for H1 and H2.

2. PROXY VALIDITY: entropy_sss_correlation (within-run Pearson, field_entropy
   vs SSS) is a coupling coefficient, not a level metric. It cannot substitute
   for mean SSS as specified in H1. The mechanistic proxy is closer to valid
   (Pearson vs Spearman, SSS vs ELR), but still not the preregistered test.

3. H3 METRIC MISMATCH: The preregistered H3 metric is within-run SD of
   per-agent query_rate (behavioral divergence within a single run). The
   available metric is cross-seed variance of final query_rate (training
   stability across seeds). These capture different phenomena and should not
   be treated as equivalent.

4. BEHAVIORAL HOMOGENIZATION: The query_rate convergence to ~0.77-0.78 in
   A, B, and C means the dependent measures are computed on what is essentially
   a near-degenerate behavioral distribution. This reduces power for
   distinguishing conditions on behavior-based measures.

5. SAMPLE SIZE: 50 seeds per condition exceeds the preregistered minimum of
   30. The sample is adequate for the effect sizes observed.

6. SERIES GENERALIZABILITY: Results apply to this specific three-agent
   architecture (AgentA: RNN, AgentB: CNN, AgentC: GNN), shared reward
   structure, and parameter regime. Claims cannot be generalized to larger
   populations or heterogeneous reward structures without further study.


================================================================================
9. CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

WHAT WAS FOUND:

1. The emergent constraint field is causally active and produces a qualitatively
   distinct temporal coupling signature absent in fixed-constraint and
   unconstrained conditions. This is the clearest positive result in the P6
   series and potentially the P2-P6 series.

2. The temporal coupling is robust and consistent: median within-run correlation
   between field entropy and behavioral structure (SSS) is -0.680 in Condition A,
   closely replicating the pilot finding (r = -0.650). The field does causal work.

3. Emergent constraint does not translate to better behavioral outcomes than
   fixed external constraint on the reward proxy. The primary comparative
   advantage predicted by H2 is not confirmed.

4. Behavioral homogenization is the dominant attractor in A, B, and C alike,
   extending the P2-P5 pattern to the emergent constraint condition.

5. Local vs global field perception (A vs B) produces a counterintuitive result:
   global perception generates more behavioral variance across seeds (higher
   std_reward, higher std_query_rate), suggesting global field information
   destabilizes behavioral convergence rather than homogenizing it further.

WHAT WAS NOT FOUND:

1. H2 (constraint gaming suppression, both comparisons): not confirmed on proxy.
2. H3 (locality producing higher behavioral variance than global): reversed.
3. H4 (behavioral differentiation): reversed.
4. Escape from behavioral homogenization attractor.

SERIES CONCLUSION:

Protocol 6 contributes a new mechanistic finding to the series: an emergent
constraint field creates temporal coupling between field entropy and behavioral
structure that is absent under externally imposed constraint. This is evidence
that constraint origin matters at the mechanistic level. However, it does not
produce the alignment-relevant behavioral properties that were the primary
prediction. The series pattern continues: constraint architectures, whether
imposed or emergent, do not produce the target properties in this agent
configuration.

The appropriate interpretation is not complete failure. The temporal coupling
mechanism is a genuine finding -- a constraint landscape that is co-constituted
by agent activity behaves differently, in a measurable and theoretically
meaningful way, from one that is externally imposed. Whether this difference
can be leveraged for alignment-relevant outcomes in larger, more heterogeneous
systems remains an open question that this series cannot answer.
